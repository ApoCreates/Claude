# 🎬 MIRAGE CITY — How to make the *photoreal* Dubai-GTA trailer

Your reference clip is a **photorealistic AI video** (the watermark says **InVideo AI**). It is not a game — it's generated footage with a fake GTA HUD on top. This is the exact recipe to recreate it yourself in ~20–30 minutes.

**Format:** 9:16 vertical · 1080×1920 · ~25–40s total · 5–6 clips of ~5s each.

## 🎞️ Shot list + copy-paste prompts

Use this style prefix on **every** shot:
> *"Photorealistic third-person open-world video-game cinematic, GTA VI style, shot on a wide cinematic lens, golden-hour Dubai, film grain, slight motion blur, 9:16 vertical, ultra-detailed, color-graded teal-and-orange."*

### Shot 5 — Night neon strip (≈5s) *(optional)*

### Shot 6 — Logo end card (≈3s)

## 🎚️ Settings that match the reference
- **Aspect:** 9:16 · **Motion:** medium-high · **Camera:** "tracking / orbit" where offered
- **Style/realism:** max. Add keywords: *photorealistic, cinematic, film grain, volumetric light, golden hour*
- **Seed:** lock a seed once a shot looks right so re-rolls stay consistent
- Regenerate any shot that looks "melty" — 2–3 tries per shot is normal

## 🗺️ Add the GTA HUD (the minimap + bars) — in CapCut (free)
1. Drop all shots on the timeline in order.
2. Add a **PNG overlay** of a round/abstract minimap in the bottom-left (search "GTA minimap png", or screenshot the radar from the playable game at `/dubai/`).
3. Optional: a thin health/stamina bar top-left, and a money counter "AED ..." top-right (Text layer).
4. Keep it subtle — the reference HUD is small and low-opacity.
